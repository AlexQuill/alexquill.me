Climbing

California - Washington - New Hampshire

Rock Climbing, specifically bouldering, is a passion I've had for nearly a decade. Bouldering routes, or "problems" as they are known for how hard it can be to unlock the right sequence of movement needed to solve them, are metrics I use to push myself and appreciate how far I've come.

I've climbed in nearly all four corners of the country, from Mt. Rainier, Washington, to Joshua Tree, California, up to Mt. Rumney, New Hampshire. As a captain of the Dartmouth Climbing team, leading practices gives me a unique opportunity to share an activity that I care deeply about with people I love.

Check out these incredible places:

Joshua Tree National Park Mount Rainier National Park Mt. Rumney

Double H Ranch

Lake Luzerne, NY

Double H Ranch is a summer camp for children ages 6-21 with life threatening illness in Lake Luzerne, New York. I have been lucky enough to spend the past two summers as a full time staff member at Double H in the Muskrat, Deer, and Wolf Cabins.

A normal day at Double H includes lots of regular camp stuff like swimming, archery, a fully accessible high ropes course, and some not so typical stuff, such as administering daily personal care routines. My summers at Double H have been the most impactful 20 weeks of my life, and have completely reshaped my hopes for my career.
